GPS for Paid Social

The same framework.
Applied to every decision we make.

GPS isn’t a reporting layer on top of paid social — it’s the structure underneath every campaign decision. Here’s how each GPS step works specifically for this channel.

G
Goal Setting: Define the business outcome before the first dollar

Social platforms optimize for whatever you tell them to optimize for. We define the KPA first — leads, appointments, applications, purchases — and structure every campaign around that signal, not the platform’s default optimization event.

→ KPA locked before audience architecture is built
P
Performance Tracking: Beyond likes, shares, and reach

Facebook Conversions API, LinkedIn Insight Tag, and GA4 configured to capture the events that actually matter to your clients. For healthcare clients: appointments booked. For B2B clients: qualified meetings. For e-commerce: revenue, not transactions.

→ Conversions API + CRM integration built before launch
S

Weekly creative analysis, audience segmentation review, and bid strategy adjustment — all measured against the defined KPA. We kill what isn’t working before it erodes the account. We scale what is.

→ Senior social specialist — same person on your accounts weekly

Common Questions

The things agencies ask
before saying yes.

Direct answers to the questions we hear most often from agencies considering adding paid social to their offering.

“Can Conduit handle creative production?”

We provide creative strategy and direction — specifying formats, hooks, CTAs, and message hierarchy in detail. Your client’s creative team or yours produces the assets. We review and approve before anything goes live. If needed, we can recommend production partners.

“What happens when Meta changes the algorithm?”

It happens constantly. Our social specialists are in the platforms daily — not weekly. Algorithm changes are identified in the optimization cycle, not the monthly report. KPA-led strategy means we adjust faster than accounts optimized to platform recommendations.

“Can you run TikTok for clients with conservative brands?”

Yes — the format and creative direction differ significantly from Meta. We map the right ad formats and content approach to the client’s brand standards before anything is produced. Conservative brands can still run effective TikTok campaigns with the right creative brief.

“How do you report social results to agency clients?”

CAC, qualified leads, appointment volume, and revenue attributed to social — in a report formatted for your client, under your brand. Not a Meta Ads Manager screenshot.
