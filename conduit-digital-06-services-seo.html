GPS for SEO

The same framework.
Applied to every decision we make.

GPS isn’t a reporting layer on top of seo — it’s the structure underneath every campaign decision. Here’s how each GPS step works specifically for this channel.

G
Goal Setting: Define what organic success looks like in revenue terms

Before we touch a title tag, we define the KPA — organic leads, organic revenue, or organic traffic to specific conversion pages. Every SEO decision flows from that business goal, not from ranking for the sake of ranking.

→ KPA defined: organic leads, revenue, or conversion pages
P
Performance Tracking: Organic visibility connected to P&L

GA4, Google Search Console, and rank tracking connected to your client’s CRM — so organic conversions are counted the same way as paid. We track what organic search actually contributes to pipeline, not just rankings.

→ GA4 + Search Console + CRM attribution built from day one
S
Systematic Optimization: Monthly progress against the KPA

Monthly: technical health check, content performance review, ranking movement analysis, and link profile monitoring — all measured against the defined KPA. We prioritize work that moves the business goal, not the one that produces the most impressive ranking report.

→ Senior SEO strategist — same specialist each month

Common Questions

The things agencies ask
before saying yes.

Direct answers to the questions we hear most often from agencies considering adding seo to their offering.

“SEO takes too long to show results — how do we sell that to clients?”

Technical wins happen within weeks. Local SEO improvements in 60–90 days. Content ranking in 3–6 months depending on authority. We set milestones before we start — so your client has a roadmap, not a promise. The GPS framework keeps expectations anchored to a defined plan.

“Can Conduit handle SEO alongside paid search for the same client?”

Yes — and it’s more effective that way. SEO data informs paid keyword strategy. Paid campaign performance informs content priorities. GA4 unifies attribution across both channels in the same monthly report.

“How do you handle content production?”

We provide content strategy, topic clusters, and detailed content briefs. Production can be handled by your client’s team, your agency’s writers, or we can recommend production partners. We review and optimize all content before it goes live.

“What happens if Google’s algorithm changes?”

It does constantly. Our SEO strategy is built around relevance and technical health — not tactics that depend on algorithm stability. When a core update rolls out, we’re reviewing its impact within 48 hours and adjusting the plan.
