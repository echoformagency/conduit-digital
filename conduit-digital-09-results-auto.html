The Situation

What was broken
before Conduit.

The dealership had been running digital advertising for three years. On paper, the numbers looked reasonable — impressions in the hundreds of thousands, CTRs at industry benchmark, and a cost-per-click that the previous vendor could point to as competitive. The agency presenting those numbers had no reason to question them. Neither did the client.

The problem was what wasn’t being measured. The previous vendor had configured Google Analytics to count page views as conversions — specifically, views of the inventory pages. Every time someone browsed the lot online, it registered as a “conversion.” Real leads — phone calls, form fills, appointment requests — were invisible in the reporting. The true cost per lead was unknown.

When the agency brought Conduit in to audit the account, the first question was simple: what’s the KPA? What does a successful month look like for the dealership’s general manager? The answer: qualified leads resulting in test drives. Not page views. Not clicks. Not even form fills — phone calls from people who had a specific vehicle in mind.

That conversation had never happened with the previous vendor. It became the foundation of everything Conduit built.

GPS on This Campaign

How Goal Performance
worked for this dealership.

Three steps. Every decision in this campaign ran through all three. Here’s what each one looked like specifically for this client.

G
Goal Setting: Define the real KPA first

Before touching the account, Conduit sat down with the agency and the dealership GM to define success: qualified phone calls from in-market buyers with a specific vehicle in mind. That became the KPA. Every campaign structure, every keyword, every creative decision flowed from that definition — not from impression share or CTR benchmarks.

P
Performance Tracking: Build attribution from scratch

GTM rebuilt. GA4 configured to track actual phone calls via CallRail, form completions with model-specific attribution, and appointment bookings from the dealer’s CRM. Every conversion event verified before launch. The previous vendor’s page-view “conversions” were removed. The real number — zero tracked leads in month one — became the baseline to build from.

S
Systematic Optimization: Weekly against the KPA

Search terms reviewed weekly. Negative keywords built from actual call recordings — if a call didn’t match buyer intent, the term that triggered it was added to the exclusion list. Social creative rotated monthly based on which inventory categories were driving calls. Programmatic layered in month three to sustain in-market audience awareness. Every change measured against cost per qualified call.

The Results

Five months.
Every metric moved.

Month one: Baseline established, not results delivered

The honest answer in month one was: we don’t know yet. The old conversions were gone. Real tracking was live but the campaigns needed time to optimize against actual call data. Conduit communicated this to the agency upfront — the first month would establish the baseline, not show the outcome. The agency held the client expectation. That transparency is part of GPS.

Month three: Cost per lead drops 71%

With three months of real call data, optimization had a signal worth following. Negative keyword lists built from call recordings were eliminating low-intent traffic. Search term reports were identifying new high-converting queries that the original campaign structure had missed. Social creative had been rotated twice based on model-specific performance data. Cost per qualified lead had dropped 71% from the month-one baseline.

Month five: The result the GM took to ownership

By month five, cost per qualified call was down 98% from the month-one baseline. Lead volume was 5× higher on the same budget. ROAS across all channels hit 3,342% — a number that connected to actual vehicle sales, not impressions delivered. The GM presented these numbers to ownership. The agency presented them at renewal. Neither conversation was difficult.
